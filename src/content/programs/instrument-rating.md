## Training Progression—Your Path to Instrument Rating

### Stage 1: IFR Fundamentals & Instrument Scan (Weeks 1-3)

**10-12 flight hours | 15+ ground hours**

- IFR regulations, airspace, and flight rules
- Instrument scan techniques (attitude instrument flying)
- Basic maneuvers under the hood (climbs, descents, turns, unusual attitudes)
- VOR, GPS, and DME navigation
- Radio communications and ATC procedures
- IFR flight planning and weather theory

**Outcome:** Solid instrument scan and basic IFR procedures proficiency.

### Stage 2: Approaches & Navigation (Weeks 4-6)

**15-18 flight hours | 12+ ground hours**

- Precision approaches (ILS with glideslope)
- Non-precision approaches (VOR, LOC, RNAV, GPS)
- Holding patterns and procedure turns
- DME arcs and complex navigation
- Missed approaches and published holds
- Partial panel (backup instruments, GPS failure scenarios)

**Outcome:** Proficiency in all approach types and navigation procedures.

### Stage 3: Real IMC Flying & Advanced Procedures (Weeks 7-9)

**15-20 flight hours (prioritize actual IMC) | 10+ ground hours**

- **Actual instrument flying in clouds** (real approaches, real weather)
- Circling approaches and visual descent points
- Complex IFR cross-country flights (multi-leg, multiple approaches)
- Airspace transitions (Class B/C operations under IFR)
- Ice, convection, and weather avoidance strategies
- Simulator sessions (cost-effective emergency practice)

**Outcome:** Confident, competent IFR pilot ready for real-world operations.

### Stage 4: Checkride Preparation & Certification (Weeks 10-12)

**5-8 flight hours | 8+ ground hours**

- Final checkride preparation with senior CFII
- Mock oral exam (scenario-based questions, systems, regulations)
- Practice checkride flights (all approaches, holds, partial panel to ACS standards)
- **FAA Written Exam** (Instrument Airplane knowledge test—pass required before checkride)
- **FAA Practical Test** with Designated Pilot Examiner
  - Oral examination (2-3 hours): IFR regulations, weather theory, systems, flight planning
  - Flight test (2-3 hours): approaches, holds, navigation, partial panel, emergency procedures

**Outcome:** **FAA Instrument Rating**—legal to fly IFR in clouds and low visibility.
